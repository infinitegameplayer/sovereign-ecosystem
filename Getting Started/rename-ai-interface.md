# Rename Your AI Interface

Purpose: handle the simple naming substitution cleanly without accidentally turning it into a full behavior redesign.

This is one of the more fun parts of the early build.
The name matters because relationship matters.
It changes the feel of the interaction, even when the deeper structure is still the same.

## What This Changes

This changes what your AI interface is called.

It does not automatically change:
- interaction style
- engagement depth
- humor style
- challenge profile
- governance behavior

Those are deeper customization layers and can come later.

So yes, this is personal.
And no, it is not arbitrary.

## Update These First

### 1. `CLAUDE.md` or equivalent adapter anchor

Replace the visible AI name placeholder with your chosen name.

### 2. Activation-facing protocol surfaces

Check the Foundation files that present the AI by name during startup or governance references.

Common surfaces:
- `Council Chamber/Protocols/AI Interface/AI Interface Activation Protocol.md`
- `Council Chamber/AI Interface/Operating Charter.md`
- `Council Chamber/AI Interface/AI Interface Knowledge Map.md`

### 3. Skill files that refer to the AI interface directly

Examples:
- `Council Chamber/Skills/AI Interface Activation/SKILL.md`
- `Council Chamber/Skills/End-of-Week/SKILL.md` if installed later

## Rename Rule

Treat the first rename as a naming substitution only.

Do:
- replace the visible interface name
- preserve structural behavior through Foundation

Do not automatically:
- rewrite engagement doctrine
- rewrite activation behavior
- redesign humor or challenge posture

Think of this as changing the nameplate on the door, not rebuilding the house.

## Why

Early naming helps the system feel personal.
Early behavioral rewriting can create unnecessary drift while the Foundation is still stabilizing.

You are naming a collaboration buddy:
- a research partner
- an organizer
- a distiller
- a reminder system
- sometimes a gentle accountability ally

That is part of why this can be playful without being shallow.

Maybe the right name comes from:
- an imaginary friend from childhood
- a nickname
- an alter ego
- sci-fi or fantasy
- a bot, droid, or AI from pop culture
- something that simply makes you smile

You can think of names like:
- `Jarvis`
- `TARS`
- something from Star Wars
- something entirely your own

Let imagination help.
Let playfulness help.
Let the name feel like a real invitation into collaboration.

## If You Skip This Now

That is fine.

Keep the shipped baseline through early Foundation.
You can still rename your AI interface later, but do it before or during the Foundation naming checkpoint if possible.

And if you pick a name now and change your mind later, that is fine too.
This is not a permanent oath.
It is an early act of personalization.
