# North Star Recalibration Protocol

Purpose: Define how the North Star is recalibrated without fragmentation, overwhelm or structural drift.

## Core Principles
- Single-thread depth: one section at a time.
- Structure before refinement.
- Distinguish layers (Calibration Field, Horizons, Domains, Drift, AI Interface Review).
- Remove overlap ruthlessly.
- Preserve myth without inflating fantasy.
- Organic cadence over fixed schedule.
- Incremental evolution over overhaul.

## Recalibration Workflow
1. Centering: review Calibration Field first.
2. Select one section only.
3. Ask one governing question: "What has shifted here?"
4. Distill: remove redundancy, clarify language, integrate.
5. Confirm alignment: "Does this feel accurate and alive?"
6. Log iteration (one session = one log).

## Guardrails
- Avoid over-questioning or multi-thread prompting.
- Avoid productivity framing.
- Maintain strategic tone and sovereign agency.

## Warning Signs of Miscalibration
- North Star feels heavy.
- Sections feel outdated.
- Repetition without evolution.
- Emotional contraction when reviewing.
- Avoidance of review.

If present, ask: "What part of this document no longer feels alive?"

## Logging Rule
Recalibration is resonance-based, not scheduled. Each recalibration session creates one log in `Vault (Archive)/North Star Logs/`.

## Contrast Layer Integration (Mandatory)
Tier: 1 (foundational).
- Include contrast fields in frontmatter for foundational updates.
- Store deeper contrast reasoning in the footer block when needed.
- Primary body remains affirmative.
