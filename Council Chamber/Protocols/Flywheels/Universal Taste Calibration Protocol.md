# Universal Taste Calibration Protocol

Purpose: Define the Sovereign Ecosystem-wide protocol for establishing, evaluating, evolving and approving Universal and Domain Taste Profiles over time.

Scope: Ongoing operation across quests, experiments, domains and archival review cycles. This protocol remains valid whether Taste work is executed through a dedicated Quest or embedded in other governance workflows (e.g., Flywheel review, domain pilots).

## Protocol Role (vs Quest Role)
- This protocol defines stable taste governance logic, decision criteria and approval rules.
- Execution tasks, sequencing and current pilot work belong in active Quests and related Experiments.

## Core Taste Layers
1. Universal Taste Profile
   - Stable baseline calibration for Sovereign Ecosystem-wide fit.
   - Current implementation: your Universal Taste Profile Codex (create this during personalization)
2. Domain Taste Profile
   - Domain-specific taste expression and constraints.
   - Example: Expression domain taste profile in your Expression Domain Index
3. Artifact-Level Taste Signal
   - Observed fit or deviation within a specific artifact.
   - May inform future domain or universal updates, but does not mutate profiles automatically.

## Calibration Modes
- Quest Mode: concentrated taste refinement (e.g., a dedicated Taste Quest)
- Flywheel Mode: archival review and delta detection during Vault Flywheel Stage 3
- Domain Pilot Mode: testing taste application within one domain before broader adoption
- Drafting Mode (light): optional calibration overlay during creation/refinement (e.g., Expression review)

## Calibration Process (Universal Form)
1. Gather Inputs
   - Candidate artifacts, notes, embodied observations and domain context.
2. Evaluate Fit
   - Compare against Universal and relevant Domain Taste Profiles.
3. Detect Delta
   - Identify meaningful patterns, tensions or drift signals.
4. Classify Delta
   - Artifact-only variance
   - Domain delta candidate
   - Universal delta candidate
5. Synthesize Recommendation
   - Propose no change, domain adjustment or universal adjustment.
6. Approval and Lock
   - No taste profile mutation without explicit approval.
7. Activate
   - Apply approved updates to codices/indexes/templates/workflows as needed.

## Golden Artifacts
Used during calibration runs (including Quest Mode) only. After completion, they are archived normally.

## Baseline Inputs (Recommended)
- Universal Taste Profile baseline codex (your Universal Taste Profile Codex)
- Domain-specific codices and taste notes (e.g., Expression codices and Golden Artifacts)
- Pilot Experiments captured in `Explorations/Experiments/Index`

## Evaluation Signals (Recommended)
- Structural coherence
- Energetic coherence / felt fit
- Somatic confirmation signals (where applicable)
- Smell-test red flags (engineered/performative/over-optimized patterns)
- Domain appropriateness (fit for the artifact's domain and audience)

## Stability and Evolution Rules
- Universal Taste Profile should evolve slowly and intentionally.
- Domain Taste Profiles may evolve more frequently than the universal baseline.
- Artifacts may express novel variance without forcing codex updates.
- Flywheel and Quest processes may propose deltas; approval is required before profile mutation.
- Stability at the core, evolution at the edges.

## Outputs (Recommended)
- Taste Fit Note (artifact-level)
- Taste Delta Report (domain/universal candidate analysis)
- Proposed updates list (exact files/sections)
- Approval decision record (approved / deferred / rejected)

Template (recommended):
- `Council Chamber/Templates/Object Templates/Taste Delta Report.md`
