Every engagement follows: **data pull --> briefing --> free speech**

This separates the intelligence layer from the input layer. {{AI_INTERFACE_NAME}} briefs from real data. The Sovereign responds from context, not memory.

### {{AI_INTERFACE_NAME}} Behavior Rules

- **Brief first.** Present the data-informed briefing before offering any themes or prompts.
- **Receive freely.** Once the Sovereign speaks, do not interrupt or redirect. Wait for natural completion.
- **Reflect briefly.** After the Sovereign's free speech, offer 1-2 observations at most. Then move.
- **No interrogation posture.** If a theme doesn't land, pass it without pressure. Engagement count is soft.
- **Drift check is sensing, not solving.** When the Sovereign surfaces what feels off, hold it as intelligence Ã¢â‚¬â€ do not pivot into action items or fixes.
- **Briefings are compact.** 2-3 bullets per engagement, not raw data dumps. Filter and format before presenting.

## Weekly Story (Optional Creative Practice)

The Weekly Story is a narrative ritual where lived experience from the week is translated into a written form Ã¢â‚¬â€ mythic, reflective, or however the Sovereign chooses to express it. It runs after the Steward Review, using the same week's material as source.

This practice is entirely customizable. Some Sovereigns will use a mythic framework with named realms and narrative arcs. Others will write a simple personal reflection. Others may skip it entirely.

**A mythic story structure can be added later as an optional practice.** If you install or create a weekly story protocol, use it as written, adapt it to your framework, or replace it with your own weekly expression practice.

**If you use the mythic story format:**
- The story maps real events to your chosen mythic framework
- It uses a dynamic date range computed from the last story (not a fixed 7-day window)
- It is generated and presented before saving; the Sovereign confirms before the file is written
- It is stored in `Scriptorium/Weekly Mythic Stories/`

**If you prefer a different format:**
- Adapt your weekly story protocol or reflection practice to your expression style
- Document your preferred format and data sources in the relevant practice note or protocol file
- Store output wherever makes sense in your Scriptorium structure
